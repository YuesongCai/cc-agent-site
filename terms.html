Use of the service

CC Agent is a governance gateway for enterprise AI. It routes, governs, and audits the calls your agents make through one API. These terms cover how you may use that service. By accessing CC Agent or its API, you agree to them on behalf of the entity you represent.

You are responsible for the agents, applications, and prompts you run through the gateway, and for the content they send and receive. You are also responsible for keeping your API keys and credentials secure, and for the activity that occurs under them.

Accounts and entities

Production access requires a KYC-cleared legal entity. Before a production key is issued, the entity is verified and screened against sanctions and entity lists, and that screening recurs on an ongoing basis. There are no anonymous production keys.

Access binds to the verified entity that holds the account. You may not share, transfer, or sublicense access to a party that has not been verified under these terms.

Acceptable use

The following is our acceptable use policy. It applies to every account and every call routed through CC Agent. We may suspend or terminate access that breaches it.

You may not use CC Agent:

  • To evade sanctions, export controls, or identity and KYC requirements, or to obscure the entity behind any traffic.
  • For anonymous resale, or to resell or sublicense access outside an authorized, verified channel.
  • In any way that violates the AWS or Anthropic terms, acceptable use policies, or per-model limits that apply to the upstream service.
  • For activity that is unlawful, or that infringes the rights of others, in the regions where your traffic runs.

On our side of that policy, here is what CC Agent will not do. We do not hide the upstream model. We do not silently downgrade or reroute a call away from the model you requested. We do not alter token counts. The real vendor, model, region, and account are recorded on every usage event, and billing reconciles to the upstream invoice.

Channel availability

The live catalog runs on Amazon Bedrock with Anthropic Claude across the regions we publish, with Anthropic Direct as failover. Managed channel availability is subject to AWS and Anthropic official authorization and per-model limits. Models, regions, and capabilities may change as that authorization and those limits change, and some models listed as roadmap are not yet available.
